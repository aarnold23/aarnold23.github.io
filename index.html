Solving Kampala’s Traffic Crisis

In 2025, Kampala’s roads, like Jinja Road and Entebbe Road, lose an estimated 1.5 million hours annually to gridlock, costing millions in fuel and emissions (KCCA, 2024). Our system, developed by Group 40, uses Raspberry Pi 4 and YOLOv5-nano to detect vehicle density and adjust traffic light timings dynamically, achieving 85% detection accuracy in a toy car prototype. This innovation could transform intersections like City Square, saving time and reducing CO2 emissions.
